In May 2024, North Korea flew rubbish-filled balloons to South Korea. Those balloons damaged several properties, with leaving nasty, smelly remarks in the region. Those were ‘dirty bombs’ verbatim. Pyongyang claimed it was a countermeasure for what they have been received from Seoul. This paper presents a simple retaliation model using a marginal utility cost model; and analyzes why North Korea chose to send rubbish balloons as a countermeasure. The model assumes the utility of retaliation decreases as stage continues, and the defender has no better options than retaliation. However, this Retaliation and counterretaliation eventually end up with total war, a critical point where the utility score turned negative. Either of both parties should gesture for ceasefire, unless they wish total war. Using the model, I suggest rubbish balloons as strategic decision not only for saving face at home and abroad; but also signaling to South Korea: leave me alone. During the conflict, Seoul attempted to escalate the situation by flying drones over Pyongyang; however, North Korea restrained themselves from retaliating in higher level but remaining their low-level retaliation. In conclusion, I suggest retaliation options can be restrained and argue that North Korea is not an unpredictable, rogue regime with aggressive behaviors; but a rational state same as other states, that their behaviors should be interpreted beyond traditional securitization paradigm. Furthermore, I suggest the model can be expanded into various conflicts, which expected to present another scope.
